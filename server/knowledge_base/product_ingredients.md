# Key Skincare Ingredient Guide

This guide explains what some of the most common and effective skincare ingredients do.

### Hyaluronic Acid (HA)
- **What it is:** A humectant, which means it draws moisture from the air into your skin.
- **Good for:** All skin types, but especially **Dry Skin**. It provides lightweight hydration for **Oily Skin** as well.
- **Benefit:** Plumps up the skin, makes it feel hydrated and supple.

### Salicylic Acid (BHA)
- **What it is:** A Beta-Hydroxy Acid. It is oil-soluble, which means it can get deep inside your pores.
- **Good for:** **Oily Skin** and **Acne-Prone Skin**.
- **Benefit:** Unclogs pores, dissolves blackheads, reduces inflammation, and acts as an exfoliant.

### Niacinamide (Vitamin B3)
- **What it is:** A powerful antioxidant and skin-restoring ingredient.
- **Good for:** All skin types, especially **Oily Skin** and **Acne-Prone Skin**.
- **Benefit:** Regulates oil production, minimizes the appearance of pores, reduces redness and inflammation, and strengthens the skin barrier.

### Retinoids (includes Retinol, Adapalene, Tretinoin)
- **What it is:** Derivatives of Vitamin A. They are considered the gold standard in anti-aging and acne treatment.
- **Good for:** **Acne-Prone Skin** and for anti-aging purposes.
- **Benefit:** Dramatically increases skin cell turnover, which unclogs pores, smooths texture, and reduces fine lines. Requires consistent use and can be irritating at first. **Sunscreen is mandatory when using retinoids.**

### Ceramides
- **What it is:** Lipids (fats) that are a natural component of your skin barrier.
- **Good for:** **Dry Skin** and **Sensitive Skin**.
- **Benefit:** They help to repair and strengthen the skin's moisture barrier, preventing water loss and protecting from irritants.
